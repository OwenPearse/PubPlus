Stage 5 — Specials, Tap Lists & Future Commerce Extensions

Purpose
Define how specials, promotions, and tap lists enrich discovery without weakening trust.

Locked outcomes

Structured specials are distinct from descriptive marketing copy.
Discovery-relevant specials must be structured; free text alone is not enough for strong discovery claims.
Recurring offers and one-off promotions are distinct patterns.
Published content, valid current content, discovery-eligible content, and active-now claims are distinct concepts.
Dynamic content has discovery qualification tiers:
detail-display safe
card/badge safe
filter/search safe
active-now/ranking safe
Weak, vague, stale, or text-dependent timing should be suppressed rather than guessed.
Tap offering state is distinct from beverage product identity.
Tap-list architecture should be lightly normalized:
venue offering state
product identity/reference
optional brewery reference
optional style/category reference
Rotating/guest/limited are offering traits, not proof of current specific-product availability.
Dynamic owner-managed content remains compatible with Stage 2 workflow rules.
Sponsored/commercial state is separate from discovery truth.

Manager refinements / approvals

Owner fast-path in this area, if ever allowed, should remain very narrow and mainly reduce/narrow/end approved claims rather than create or broaden new discovery-facing claims.
Event-linked promos, redemption, bookings, boosted placements, and campaign logic should attach as adjacent layers, not merge into Stage 5 truth.

Main guardrails

No vague promo blob.
No collapsing recurring and one-off logic.
No published-implies-active-now shortcut.
No raw tap text as strong discovery truth.
No coupling of commerce/redemption/booking logic into the core truth model.
