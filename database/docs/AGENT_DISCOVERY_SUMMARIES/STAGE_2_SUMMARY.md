Stage 2 — Moderation, Staging & Provenance

Purpose
Define how PubPlus safely moves from messy mixed-source input into trustworthy published truth.

Locked outcomes

PubPlus uses a four-layer model:
Raw/source
Proposed/staged
Reviewed/decisioned
Published
No actor writes directly into the published layer except through the formal publish workflow.
Proposal model is hybrid:
whole-record proposals
field-family proposals
Publishability is checked at both record level and field-family level, with stricter rules for high-risk domains.
Provenance, freshness, evidence basis, and auditability are first-class.
Rollback is lineage-preserving: restore prior published truth where defensible, otherwise withhold.
Review lifecycle is explicit and stateful, not vague/informal.
Actor types remain distinct: source/import, user, owner, admin.

Manager refinements / approvals

Verified owners may have a very narrow structured fast-path, but only for approved low-ambiguity fields.
User submissions never bypass review.
High-risk families include hours/open-now, geography/location, structured discovery claims, and active specials/promotions.
High-risk fields may publish a lower-risk subcomponent while withholding a stronger derived claim.
Every meaningful decision should retain an internal reason/evidence basis.

Main guardrails

Submission is not truth.
Workflow history is not published truth.
No silent overwrite of live data.
No vague stale/unknown/rejected collapse.
No rollback that destroys history or republishes weak truth by accident.
